Projects

Toy Camera
February 2020

My group of 3 fabricated a toy camera for our Intro to Making course. We assembled a frame from laser cut and 3D printed pieces and used an Arduino, electronics, and LEDs to replicate a real camera's functions. I was in charge of programming the electronics and assembling the circuits.

Chiptune/8-Bit Effect
Fall 2019

Using Nyquist, a sound synthesis and composition language, I wrote a chiptune/8-bit effect library. Users can use the library to change audio into chiptune and mix the original and chiptune sounds.

Language Identification System
Fall 2019

For our Speech Recognition course project, I collaborated with two teammates to design a successful language identification system for English, German, and Mandarin Chinese speech. The system used phones for identifying the language. We developed the system and baseline comparisons using Python in Google Colab.

Window Washing Robot
Spring 2019

For my undergraduate capstone, my team of 5 developed an autonomous robot that adheres to and cleans windows. The robot automatically navigates over a window pane, crosses over a barrier, and cleans a 5' x 6' total area in under 3 minutes. I was in charge of assembling the electronic components and circuitry of the robot. I also helped to develop the control, locomotion, adhesion, sensing, and cleaning subsystems.

Dialog System
November 2018

One of the course projects for my Speech Processing class was to build a multi-turn pizza ordering system as an Alexa Skill for Amazon Echos. I created state diagrams and used ngrok and flask-ask to develop servers and dialog for the Alexa skill.
