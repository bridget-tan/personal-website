Event Production & Audio Engineering

In my freshman year of college, I joined the student organization AB Tech, which provides our campus with professional technical production. I joined with no prior experience, but it soon became one of my most valued experiences. From working on open mics to talent shows to concerts and orientation, I've been able to take on many different roles. My primary focus is in sound, rigging, and management, but I'm also interested in lighting.



AB Tech is where I found a passion for audio engineering. You can usually find me in the house as the Front of House engineer or backstage as the monitors engineer. I've worked sound for various events like Greek Sing and concerts featuring acts like Nghtmre, Snakehips, Gnash, and Smallpools. My favorite part about being a sound engineer is mixing live.

I am also a master rigger for AB Tech. As a rigger, I oversee the safety and implementation of flown equipment. I've worked on shows like Lunar Gala and various indoor and outdoor concerts.

As a senior member of the org, I've also run events as a production manager, or as we call Techie-in-Charge. The largest event I've supervised is CMU's 2019 First Year Orientation. I love to see events come together and become successful even though it was stressful or daunting.

Some of my favorite experienes in Tech are: being an assistant FoH for my first concert featuring Nghtmre, and lighting a dance that the audience "ooh'd" at! Working as a student technician has helped me to appreciate all who work in the background.

Chinese Culture

I am very passionate about Chinese culture. Before college, I went to Chinese school every Sunday and my favorite parts were the extracurricular activities afterwards. I practiced martial arts, learned Chinese Yo-Yo, did Chinese dance, and more. My favorite activity, however, was lion dance, which I got to perform many times at libraries and events. Fun fact: my favorite holiday is Chinese New Year, because of all the great food and traditions like lion dance!

In college, I became involved with Awareness of Roots in Chinese Culture (ARCC), a student organization dedicated to sharing Chinese culture to the campus community. Through ARCC, I was able to host events celebrating Chinese holidays like the Mid-Autumn Festival, and Chinese food like hot pot. In Spring semesters, we also put on a play based on a Chinese folktale and showcasing traditional and modern dances, music, and more. I have been deeply involved with all aspects of this show, and have been in several dances. I've also had the privilege of choreographing lion dance for the show twice. It was a joy to teach others a tradition that I treasure greatly.


Environment

I care a lot about the environment. Yearly outings like going camping, skiing, and visiting national parks have made me appreciate the world we live in. Part of the reason why I like to do nature photography is being able to share the wonderful outdoors as I see it. I've volunteered a lot, such as cleaning rivers of trash, sorting recyclables for my high school, and more. As part of a leadership program at CMU, I also helped to spread awareness about recycling during my freshman year. Although I am focusing on my other passions now, I still try my best to be environmentally conscious.
